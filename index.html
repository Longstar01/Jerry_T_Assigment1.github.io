My Interest in IT:

What is your interest in IT? When did your interest in IT start? Was there a particular event or person that sparked your interest? Outline your IT experience (if any).

I was interested in Information Technology (IT) in my early teens derived from my fascination with technology and the vast repository of information that is the internet. However, I only started to consider it as a course I wanted to pursue during my foundation year at Trinity College of the University of Melbourne. I was initially going for a bachelor of Mechanical Engineering, but after a semester, I understood I was not meant for the field. I have discussed it with my senior, which opened my eyes into the world of IT and founded a new goal to work myself towards.

First IT job started when I was in my late teens, and have been in IT ever since still bouncing around different areas.

Why did you choose to come to RMIT?

Initially, my plan for University was to be studying at the University of Melbourne. However, through further research and talking to some seniors studying a course similar to what I was aiming for, made me realise the teaching style and learning environment was not what I wanted. A friend that was studying at RMIT informed about his experiences doing his Masters Engineering degree. This made me want to consider my course in RMIT.

What do you expect to learn during your studies?

I expect to learn a variety of skills that would assist in my future as someone planning to work in the IT industry. One of the biggest things I hope to learn is team management and efficient project development. The practical experience and hands-on knowledge that RMIT provides in its courses would give me a significant advantage when I head into the job market.
